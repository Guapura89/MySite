This is a section with some stories of terrifying places in diferent points of the world. Stories have been told that paranormal things happen in these places, like screaming or things like that, since they are frequently visited by travelers.

One of the most terrifying places is in our country and it is the Island of the dolls. This island gives even the bravest in the world goose bumps. If you dare to visit it, more than 1000 dolls hanging from trees will observe you during the tour. It is a heartbreaking and terrifying panorama in equal measure. Legend has it that shortly after a girl drowned in the channels of Xochimilco, Don Julián Santana Barrera -the manager of the island- found a doll floating in the water. As a show of respect and to calm the spirit of the drowned woman, he decided to hang her from a tree. Since then and for 50 years he has been hanging dolls around the island; until he himself drowned in the same place where the girl died ... creepy!

A scary place is to enter a dark cave. In the case of the Actun Tunichil Muknal cave, in the Cayo district, the visit is even more terrifying, since you have to swim first to get to the chilling part. A few meters away, you find human skeletons scattered on the ground that are believed to have been sacrifices made by the Mayans to honor the gods of the underworld. The best known human remains in the cave are those of the "Crystal Maiden", a young adolescent who lies face up on the ground and her calcified bones glow with intensity from the calcite that covers them.

The necropolises of the world are terrifying and are always strewn with gloomy legends. One of the most terrifying places in the world is the Chauchilla cemetery. It is a desert area in the department of Ica in Peru, the visit becomes even more sinister not only because the grave robbers have scattered part of the human remains throughout the area, but because many tombs have been left open and the Pre-Inca skeletons watch you from their places for eternal rest, in a fetal position and wrapped in their mortuary cloth.

The inhospitable area of Atacama housed for many years a small population around a nitrate mine in which men, women and children were exploited to death during extraction. Today, decaying and wrapped in stories of paranormal events, Humberstone is a ghost town - declared a World Heritage Site - that very few dare to visit. If you walk through its streets you will be able to see the decline of a town that at the end of the 19th century boasted wealth and life. Some emblematic buildings such as the theater, the church or the hotel are still standing. But you have to be careful, they say that you hear noises and you can feel the presence of the miners who died for that "white gold". Without a doubt it is a spooky scene, only for horror lovers, it is considered one of the most terrifying places in the world. .

The most terrifying places in the world are in mysteriously strategic corners of the world. The Gran Hotel Viena is wrapped in ghost stories and fables, this hotel near Mar Chiquita in the province of Córdoba is considered one of the places with the most paranormal activity in all of Latin America. It was built in the forties as a luxury hotel, it is believed that it was even a refuge for the Nazis and Adolf Hitler himself. Today it is a museum and many visitors claim to have seen apparitions such as that of the former head of security, women standing by room windows or girls running through the corridors.

On the shores of the Epecuén lagoon, in the province of Buenos Aires, this luxury tourist village was created in the mid-20th century. But in 1986 the entire town was covered by water after a strong flood that broke the embankment ... and it was like that for two decades! All kinds of superstitions were created around this submerged town, until one day the water evaporated completely, bringing to light its former splendor, now turned into a gloomy and gloomy panorama. Of its streets and buildings now only dark traces of a luxurious past remain. Many visitors have heard screams.
