Musical genres

Hip hop

hip-hop is a culture and art movement that was created by African Americans, Latino Americans and Caribbean Americans in the Bronx, New York City. The origin of the name is often disputed. It is also argued as to whether hip hop started in the South or West Bronx. hip hop is characterized by four key elements: "rapping" (also called MCing or emceeing).

Classic music

It is a musical genre based on the liturgical and secular musical traditions of the West, especially in Europe. And the most relevant interpreters or authors were: Wagner, Bach, Mozart, Beethoven and Chopin. The trajectory began in the 11th century and continues until now, but its most relevant period spans 400 years from the mid-16th century to the early 20th century.

Jazz

We can say that jazz is characterized mainly by: improvisation, the treatment of rhythm, the use of offbeat notes and syncopations. One of the greatest jazz musicians was the trumpeter Louis Armstrong, but artists such as Les Paul, Ray Charles or Tito Puente are also known throughout the world, as well as the well-known Frank Sinatra.

Country

Traditional country is characterized by the use of certain string instruments such as the guitar, the banjo, the simple violin and the double bass. The accordion and harmonica also frequently intervened. Some of its best known representatives are: Willie Nelson, Johnny Cash, Patsy Cline or Garth Brooks.

Funk

It was born thanks to some soul artists such as James Brown who during the 60s developed a more rhythmic and energetic style with very powerful bass lines. This genre was the starting point for many new styles of dance music such as disco. George Clinton, Jamiroquai, Prince and Maceo Parker represent this musical style very well.

Reggae

It originated in Jamaica thanks to the influence of ska and rocksteady, but also by other styles such as blues or jazz. However, in this case the song lyrics are linked to social criticism and address all kinds of topics. Bob Marley was the one who popularized this genre and the highest representative of it. But we can't stop citing others like: Los Cafres, Lee Perry, Jimmy Cliff and Gregory Isaacs.

Metal

It is a musical genre that was born in the mid-sixties and early seventies in the United Kingdom and the United States, whose origins come from blues rock, hard rock and psychedelic rock. It is one of the most controversial types of music as many distinguish Traditional Heavy Metal from a more general Heavy Metal.

Rock

It began in the 60s in the United Kingdom and uses different instruments, the most common are: bass, drums, electric guitar and keyboard. Its creation arose thanks to the type of music known as Rhyhm and Blues and Country as well as influences from the folk and blues. Some of the most popular bands: Queen, The Beatles, Guns and Roses, AC / DC or Nirvana.

House

House is a purely electronic genre whose main influences are electro, synthpop, hi-NRG and electronics, although it is also influenced by the more soul and funk variants of disco music. Chicago became popular in the early 80s and is one of the types of electronic music that has evolved the most and has given more subgenres since its birth.
